F-15J Eagle

Air superiority fighter — Japan / Mitsubishi Heavy Industries (overview)

F-15J (Japanese: F-15J イーグル) is an air superiority fighter operated by the Japan Air Self-Defense Force (JASDF). Manufactured under license by Mitsubishi Heavy Industries, it is a Japanese variant of the McDonnell Douglas F-15 Eagle. The aircraft serves as Japan's primary air defense fighter, featuring advanced radar systems and air-to-air capabilities.

History

Japan selected the F-15 Eagle in 1978 as part of its modernization program. Licensed production began in 1981, with Mitsubishi leading domestic manufacture. Early aircraft were assembled from imported parts, with local content increasing in later production. The type has undergone several upgrade programs to maintain its effectiveness.

Design

Avionics

The F-15J features a Japanese-developed J/APG-1 radar system, later upgraded to J/APG-2 in some aircraft. Modern variants include improved electronic warfare systems, Link 16 datalinks, and advanced mission computers. The cockpit incorporates both Japanese and American display systems.

Performance

Powered by two Pratt & Whitney F100 turbofan engines, the F-15J can achieve speeds up to Mach 2.5. The aircraft features excellent acceleration and high-altitude performance. Its large fuel capacity and conformal fuel tanks provide extended range for patrol missions.

Armament

Standard armament includes an M61A1 20mm cannon and various air-to-air missiles including the AAM-3, AAM-4, and AAM-5. The aircraft can carry up to eight missiles in various configurations. Modern upgrades have improved its beyond-visual-range combat capabilities.

Service

The F-15J entered JASDF service in 1981 and serves as Japan's primary air defense fighter. They are stationed at several air bases across Japan and maintain continuous alert status. The fleet has been modernized through various upgrade programs to extend its service life.

Variants

  • F-15J: Single-seat fighter version
  • F-15DJ: Two-seat training version
  • F-15J Kai: Modernized variant
  • F-15J MSI: Multi-Stage Improvement upgraded version
