Type 16 Maneuver Combat Vehicle

Wheeled tank destroyer — Japan / Mitsubishi Heavy Industries (overview)

Type 16 The Type 16 maneuver combat vehicle (16式機動戦闘車, Hitoroku shiki kidō sentōsha) is a wheeled armored fighting vehicle of the Japan Ground Self-Defense Force (JGSDF). The Type 16 MCV equips designated combat units. Due to its light weight and small size, it is designed for easy deployment (by aircraft if needed) allowing rapid movement on narrow roads and in built-up areas in response to various contingencies. Despite its small size and light armor, it can successfully attack much larger armored fighting vehicles as well as personnel, using its large caliber gun

Design

Armament

The main armament is a Japan Steel Works 105mm rifled gun with an autoloader system. It can fire various ammunition types including APFSDS rounds. Secondary armament includes a coaxial 7.62mm machine gun and a 12.7mm remote weapon station. The fire control system features modern thermal imaging and laser rangefinding capabilities.

Mobility

The Type 16 uses an 8×8 wheeled configuration powered by a 570hp diesel engine. It can achieve highway speeds up to 100 km/h and features selectable all-wheel drive modes. The vehicle's relatively light weight allows it to use bridges and roads that cannot support heavier tracked vehicles.

Protection

The welded steel armor provides protection against small arms fire and shell splinters. Additional modular armor can be fitted for increased protection. The vehicle includes NBC protection systems and modern battlefield networking capabilities.

Service

The Type 16 entered service with JGSDF units starting in 2016. It serves primarily with rapid deployment forces and units responsible for defending Japan's islands. The vehicle complements rather than replaces existing tanks, filling a unique mobility-focused role.
